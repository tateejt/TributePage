The Life of Martin Luther King Jr.

The Life of MLK:

  • 1929 - Michael Luther King Jr. was born
  • 1944 - King enrolls at Morehouse College after passing the entrance exam at age 15.
  • 1946 - The Atlanta Constitution publishes a letter to the editor from King supporting minority rights.
  • 1958 - King and other civil rights leaders meet with President Dwight Eisenhower in Washington.
  • 1960 - King moves from Montgomery to Atlanta to focus on the civil rights struggle.
  • 1961 - King and hundreds of others are arrested in desegregation campaign in Albany, Ga.
  • 1965 - King gives his first speech against the Vietnam War.
  • 1968 - March 23: King leads 6,000 protesters in support of striking sanitation workers in Memphis. The march ends with violence and looting.
  • 1968 - April 3: King returns to Memphis, intending to lead a peaceful march. At an evening rally, he delivers his final speech, “I’ve Been to the Mountaintop.”
  • 1968 - April 4: King is shot and killed on the balcony of the Lorraine Motel.
